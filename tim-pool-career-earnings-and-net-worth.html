Tim Pool career earnings and net worth

Timothy Daniel Pool (born March 9, 1986) is a YouTuber, political pundit, and podcast host from the United States. He rose to prominence after live filming the 2011 Occupy Wall Street protests. In 2014, he joined Vice Media and Fusion TV before going solo on YouTube and other platforms.

Pool bought a one-way bus ticket to New York after seeing a viral video from Occupy Wall Street. Pool joined the Occupy Wall Street protestors on September 20, 2011, and soon after met Henry Ferry, a former realtor, and sales manager, with whom he created The Other 99, a media company. Pool also started live streaming the protests on his phone and immediately took on an on-camera role. While reporting on Occupy Wall Street, Pool used a live chat stream to reply to viewer questions. Pool also allowed his audience to direct where he shot the film. He converted a toy remote-controlled Parrot AR. Drone for aerial surveillance and customized software for live streaming into a DroneStream system.

Pool offered nonstop 21-hour coverage of Occupy Wall Street’s eviction from Zuccotti Park in mid-November 2011. Pool’s usage of live streaming footage and overhead drones during Occupy Wall Street events in 2011 prompted a Guardian piece to question whether such operations may be considered unproductive spying. A masked assailant approached him physically in January 2012. Following a conflict between Pool and Ferry, The Other 99 was also disbanded in January 2012. Pool also intended to webcast occupy protests across the United States for a documentary called Occumentary, but the project was never completed.
