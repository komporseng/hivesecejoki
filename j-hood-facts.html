Singer’s Bio – Who is J Hood?

J Hood was born in Brooklyn, New York on February 4, 1985. His real name is Jevon Hillman, who is a singer, rapper, and songwriter that made his debut in the music scene in 2010. Growing up in the projects of Brooklyn, J Hood was heavily influenced by various types of music genres, including soul, pop, R&B, and hip-hop.

Career Journey

J Hood started his music career as part of the rap group “The Lox” in the early 2000s, which included members Sheek Louch, Styles P, and Jadakiss. However, J Hood left the group in 2005, citing artistic differences. In 2006, he signed with D-Block Records and released his debut album, “Hoodlivin: The Mixtape,” which was well-received by fans. In 2010, J Hood released his first album “The Book of J,” and in 2015, he released “Empty Album: The Scars of an Emcee.”

Since then, J Hood has been consistently releasing new music and collaborations with other artists in the industry. In October 2021, he released a new single titled “Torn,” which has been making waves in the music scene.
